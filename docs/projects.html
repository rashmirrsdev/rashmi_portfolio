How much of a portfolio's return is actually skill?

Completed · June 2026

I decomposed three years of Nifty 50 returns into market-driven and stock-specific components. The result: 91% of the portfolio's 30.97% return came from just owning the index. The remaining 9% — the part that stock selection actually contributed — had a Sharpe of 0.21, against the market's 0.71. The analysis also shows why Beta shifts during a crisis, and why diversification by position count isn't the same as diversification by risk factor.

What Greeks tell you that position size doesn't

Completed · June 2026

A derivatives risk monitor for a 12-position NSE options book. One position — not the largest by notional — held 45% of the portfolio's total directional exposure. Position-size view missed it entirely. The project also shows what a -10% market crash looks like before it happens (the Vega exposure partially offsets the Delta loss), and why historical VaR and parametric VaR give different answers for the same portfolio.

Portfolio Analytics Dashboard

In Progress · June 2026

A Python tool that ingests a portfolio of assets and computes: daily returns, cumulative P&L, Sharpe ratio, Sortino ratio, maximum drawdown, rolling beta, Calmar ratio, and Value at Risk. Built with pandas, matplotlib, and yfinance. The metrics mirror what quant analysts, portfolio managers, and risk teams use daily — not textbook output but production-style analytics.

P&L Attribution Tool

Planned · August 2026

Replicates the P&L attribution methodology used in trading risk management: decomposing daily P&L into Delta P&L, Gamma P&L, Vega P&L, Theta decay, and unexplained residual. This is exactly how derivatives desks report performance — breaking down what drove the number so traders and risk managers can understand it. Built in Python with a clean tabular output format.
