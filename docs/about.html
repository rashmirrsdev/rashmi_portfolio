About

Quantitative finance professional · Morgan Stanley · IIT Madras

I'm Rashmi — a quantitative finance professional based in Mumbai.

I work in Trading Risk Management at Morgan Stanley, where I've spent the last three years building and operating risk analytics frameworks across one of the firm's most complex portfolios: a large, diverse book of commodity derivatives spanning crude oil, EU power and natural gas, LME metals, and agricultural products across global markets.

Day to day, that means P&L attribution, derivatives exposure analytics, risk sensitivities (Delta, Vega, Gamma, Theta), and monitoring live market moves across 20+ instruments using Bloomberg. I also build the tools the team uses — Python, SQL, Power BI — and present risk and performance findings at weekly forums with Director and MD-level stakeholders.

Before finance, I was at IIT Madras, where I completed an MS Research in Engineering Design (CGPA 9.56/10), published four peer-reviewed papers in international journals and conferences, and taught a class of 80+ students as a teaching assistant. My research focused on machine learning methods for large dataset analysis and visualisation.

Why I write here

I write about quantitative finance — the concepts I work with every day, the tools I find useful, and the things I wish someone had explained to me earlier. The goal is to make technical finance topics accessible, with examples grounded in how markets and portfolios actually behave rather than textbook abstractions.

I also keep a separate section for students at IIT Madras going through campus placements. In 2023 I went through the same process as an MS Research scholar, and I documented the journey in real time. That series is still up and still gets read — it's written for people in exactly that situation.

What I'm working on next

I'm building a set of open-source Python tools in quantitative finance: a portfolio analytics dashboard, a commodity market analytics tool, a P&L attribution calculator, and a derivatives pricing engine. Each one is grounded in methodology I use professionally. They'll be published on GitHub as they're completed — see the Projects page for the full list.

If you'd like to connect — whether you're a recruiter, a student thinking about finance, or someone working in the same space — find me on LinkedIn or write to me at rashmi.ramasushil@gmail.com.
