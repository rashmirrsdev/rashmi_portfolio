The journey

Sep 2020 — Jun 2023
MS Research · IIT Madras
Engineering Design. CGPA 9.56/10. ML research on large-scale dataset analysis. 4 publications. ASME essay competition winner. Teaching assistant for 80+ students.
Dec 2022
Campus placement — 3 offers in one day
Bajaj, JP Morgan & Chase, and Morgan Stanley all offered on Day 1. Accepted Morgan Stanley for the quant/analytical angle and the exposure to live markets. Full story in the For Students section.
Jul 2023 — Dec 2024
Associate · Trading Risk Management · Morgan Stanley
Built 30+ risk reporting tools monitoring $50M–$300M notional across 5 global commodity desks. Developed derivatives analytics (Greeks, P&L attribution). 40% reduction in manual workload through Python automation.
Jan 2025 — Present
Senior Associate · Trading Risk Management · Morgan Stanley
Leads P&L and risk review forums with Director/MD-level stakeholders. Monitors 20+ instruments daily via Bloomberg across oil, EU power, gas, LME metals, and agricultural markets. Drives analytical tool development.
Building now
Quant finance projects + CFA
Portfolio analytics, commodity market analytics, P&L attribution tool, derivatives pricer. CFA Level 1 complete. Targeting an analytical quant or portfolio analytics role next.

What actually transferred from research to finance

🧠

Problem structuring

Breaking down a complex, ambiguous problem into tractable parts. Same skill whether you're writing a paper or explaining a P&L move to an MD.

🐍

Python & data skills

Immediately useful. Building tools from scratch — not just using existing ones — made a real difference early. Research had made this second nature.

📊

Communicating quantitative findings

Presenting findings to non-technical audiences is a core research skill. In finance, it's presenting risk narratives to traders and senior stakeholders.

If you have a strong quantitative background and are considering finance: the analytical skills transfer. The finance knowledge is learnable. What matters is the ability to think rigorously, build things that work, and communicate findings clearly. The domain catches up — the foundation doesn't.
