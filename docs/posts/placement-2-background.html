2 — My Background

Rashmi Rama Sushil · April 2023 · 3 min read

As part of the IIT Madras placement process, we were required to submit a comprehensive master resume — a longer document containing all relevant information before trimming it down to the final resume sent to companies.

My background, briefly:

Education

  • MS Research, Engineering Design — IIT Madras (2020–2023). CGPA 9.56/10. Research focus: machine learning methods for large dataset analysis and visualisation. Published four papers (Springer, ASME, WCSMO, ACSMO). Won the ASME IDETC International Essay Competition in 2022.
  • Undergraduate in Mechanical Engineering. GATE 2020 — 98.31 percentile (how I got into IIT Madras for the MS programme).

Skills at the time of placements

  • Python (self-taught during preparation — see Post 4)
  • Machine learning: supervised and unsupervised methods, neural networks, dimensionality reduction
  • Statistics: regression, probability, distributions
  • Research and academic writing

What I did not have

No CS degree, no internship in data science or finance, no prior coding experience before July 2022. I was targeting data science, analytics, and quant roles from a pure research background. That context matters for reading the rest of this series.

The master resume approach

The master resume contains everything — every project, every publication, every skill, every achievement. The final resume sent to each company is then a curated subset of that master document, tailored to what that specific company is looking for. This makes it much easier to customise quickly without forgetting anything.

The template I used organised the master resume section by section: education, research experience, publications, skills, projects, achievements, and extracurriculars. For each section, I listed everything first, then marked which items were most relevant for each target company type.
