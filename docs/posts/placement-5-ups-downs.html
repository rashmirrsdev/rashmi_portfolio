Placement Journey

5 — Ups and Downs

Rashmi Rama Sushil · April 2023 · 4 min read

The highlight of my placement journey — if you can call it that — was its timing: right at the start of the biggest technical recession the world had ever faced. The uncertainty of whether companies would hire at all, combined with my being a newcomer in the field of data science, made for a genuinely anxious few months.

The placement tests were unpredictable. Some companies had five sections, others had two. Some asked high-level aptitude, others focused heavily on coding. A significant number of tests happened offline — you had to travel to a designated building, supervised by the placement team, sometimes taking three tests in a single day. It was exhausting.

When things started to shift

I started to see a ray of hope in the last 10 days before the placement week. My first interview shortlist came in for an AI Researcher position at Honda — an international profile, a strong company, and genuinely exciting. After months of test after test with very little feedback, getting shortlisted felt significant.

Then, in the final few days, things accelerated. I was shortlisted for General Electric, Bajaj, Exxon Mobil, and JP Morgan & Chase. And just 10 hours before Day 0, Morgan Stanley shortlisted me.

What the downs actually felt like

I don't want to gloss over the difficult part. There were weeks in October and November where I was taking test after test, doing my best, and not getting through. You don't get feedback. You don't know if the problem is your coding, your aptitude, your resume, or just the sheer volume of applicants. The not-knowing is the hardest part.

What helped me: staying focused on what I could control (preparation, revision, consistency), not comparing my timeline to others, and remembering that placement is partly a numbers game — the more tests you take, the more chances you create.

The lesson

The last-minute shortlists — including Morgan Stanley with less than 10 hours' notice — reinforced something I'd heard but hadn't fully believed: the timeline is not linear, and things can change very fast at the end. Keep preparing until it's over. Don't read into silence as rejection.
